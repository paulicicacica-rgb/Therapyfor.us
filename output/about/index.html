About Us

We built TherapyFor.us because finding help shouldn't be the hardest part.

Most people who need therapy never start looking — not because they don't want help, but because the search itself feels overwhelming. We try to make that first step smaller.

TherapyFor.us is an independent guide to online therapy. We write about the real situations that bring people to therapy — divorce, grief, burnout, anxiety, the quiet loneliness of starting over in a new country — and point people toward licensed help that can actually meet them there.

We partner with BetterHelp, one of the largest online therapy platforms in the US, and we may earn a commission if you sign up through our links. That doesn't change what we write or how we write it — see our Editorial Standards for how we keep that separation honest.

Alin, Founder
Built TherapyFor.us after seeing how hard it was for immigrants and people outside the mainstream to find mental health support that actually understood their situation.

What we believe

  • Therapy works, and the biggest barrier is usually just starting.
  • Mental health content should never exaggerate symptoms or use fear to drive clicks.
  • People searching for help deserve information written with care, not just keywords.
  • Affordability and access matter — therapy shouldn't be a luxury.

What we're not

We are not a clinic, a crisis service, or a substitute for professional medical advice. We don't provide therapy ourselves. We're a guide that helps you find it — through BetterHelp's network of licensed therapists, or through other resources if that's not the right fit for you.

If you're in crisis right now

Please call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), available 24/7. This site is not equipped to handle emergencies.
