The barrier isn't just money—it's the entire system designed around people with time and resources. Online therapy removes most of those barriers. No commute. Questions people ask before starting

Will online therapy actually work, or is it second-best because I can't afford in-person?
Online therapy is equally effective for most issues—anxiety, depression, relationship stress, grief. Your therapist can't prescribe medication through a screen, but they can help you think differently, build skills, and feel less alone. The research is clear: it's not a compromise. It's a real option.
How much does this actually cost in Louisiana?
Most affordable online therapy runs $30-$80 per week, depending on the platform and therapist. That's roughly $120-$320 per month. Many platforms offer 20% off your first month. You pay weekly or monthly—no surprise bills. Far less than a single traditional therapy session.
What if I start therapy and realize my therapist isn't a good fit?
You can switch anytime, typically for free. Most platforms let you request a different therapist without penalty or explanation. Finding the right fit matters, and reputable services know that. You shouldn't feel trapped with someone who doesn't get you.
Do I need to use my insurance or tell anyone about this?
No. Online therapy platforms that work without insurance keep everything private and confidential. There's no claim going to an insurance company, no record with your employer. It's just between you and your therapist. Your privacy is protected.
Will talking to a therapist on my computer really help, or do I need to sit in an office?
The therapeutic relationship—the real conversation—happens the same way on video or in person. Many people actually open up more easily from home, where they feel safe. If you prefer in-person eventually, you can transition. But video therapy works. Thousands of Louisianans are proof.
