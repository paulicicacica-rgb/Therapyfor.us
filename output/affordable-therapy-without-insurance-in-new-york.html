Questions people ask before starting

Is online therapy as good as seeing someone in person?
Studies show they're equally effective for most situations. Many people actually prefer it—no commute, no waiting room, and you can be in a space where you feel safe and comfortable. The relationship with your therapist matters more than the format.
What if I can't afford therapy, period?
Online therapy platforms like BetterHelp offer sessions starting at around $60-90 per week—roughly what a fancy coffee habit costs. You can also ask about sliding scale rates or payment plans. Some therapists work on a reduced fee basis specifically for people without insurance.
How much does it actually cost per week?
Most plans run between $65-90 per week for weekly sessions. That's significantly less than traditional therapy in New York. Plus, new members often get 20% off their first month, which makes the first few weeks even more accessible while you figure out if it's working for you.
What if therapy doesn't actually help me?
It's worth trying for at least 4-6 sessions with the same therapist—that's when you usually start seeing shifts. But if you're not feeling it, you can switch therapists immediately at no penalty. This isn't a contract; it's a service designed around you feeling better.
What if I can't open up to a stranger about personal stuff?
That feeling is completely normal and incredibly common. Therapists know this. The first session isn't about deep revelations—it's about seeing if you click. You can test the waters, ask questions, and decide if this person is right for you. And if they're not, you switch. Your comfort matters.
If you are in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 immediately — the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. BetterHelp is not a crisis service.
