You grew up performing, not belonging

Your childhood wasn't about your needs. It was about managing someone else's moods, protecting a parent's ego, or keeping the peace at any cost. You became hyperaware. You learned to anticipate. You learned that your feelings were secondary—or in the way. This wasn't a small thing. It shaped how you see yourself, how you trust others, and whether you even know what you actually want.

Now, years later, you're still doing it. Still reading the room. Still asking permission to exist. Still second-guessing your own perception of reality. You might feel guilty for having needs. You might struggle to leave relationships that hurt you. You might swing between people-pleasing and explosive anger, with no middle ground. None of this is a character flaw. It's what happens when a child has to survive in an environment where their parent's needs eclipse everything else.

I didn't even realize I was still waiting for permission to be happy. That was the moment everything changed.

The worst part is the confusion. You might not even call what happened 'abuse'—maybe your parent wasn't violent. Maybe they were charming to everyone else. Maybe you have good memories mixed in with the painful ones. That contradiction doesn't make it less real. Emotional neglect, manipulation, conditional love, and having your reality questioned—these leave marks. And right now, you're carrying those marks into every relationship, every decision, every quiet moment alone.

Why this is so hard to untangle alone

You can't think your way out of what you learned in your body. Your nervous system was wired during childhood to stay alert, to manage, to disappear. Willpower won't rewire that. Self-help books won't convince you that your needs matter. You need someone outside the system to help you see the pattern, name what happened, and slowly rebuild trust—in yourself, in others, in your own judgment.

Therapy works because it creates safety to feel what you've been holding. A therapist trained in this specific wound understands that you're not broken. You're adaptive. You developed survival skills that protected you. Now you need to decide which ones still serve you and which ones are keeping you stuck. That's not a quick fix. But it's real, and it's possible.

What helps

Therapy for adult children of narcissists focuses on reconnecting you with your own needs, rebuilding your sense of reality, and learning to set boundaries without guilt. Over time, you'll move from managing others' emotions to managing your own life. This shifts how you choose relationships, how you speak to yourself, and how much peace you have.

Questions people ask before starting

Won't therapy just make me blame my parent? I don't want to be angry at them.
Therapy isn't about blame—it's about clarity. You can understand what happened, feel your real feelings about it, and still choose your relationship with that parent. Many people find that naming the wound actually softens the anger, because you're no longer confused.
What if I'm not sure what I actually feel? I'm out of touch with my own emotions.
That's incredibly common and exactly why therapy helps. Your therapist will help you slow down and notice what's happening in your body, what you're avoiding, what you're protecting. Reconnecting with your emotions is part of the healing.
How much does this cost? Can I afford weekly sessions?
Through BetterHelp, therapy starts at just $60-90 per week depending on your match and needs. And we offer 20% off your first month. You get access to licensed therapists without the hassle of insurance or high out-of-pocket costs.
Will online therapy actually work for something this deep?
Yes. Hundreds of adults have found real healing through online therapy for exactly this issue. You work from wherever feels safe, on your schedule. The relationship with your therapist is what heals you, and that builds the same way—online or in an office.
What if the first therapist isn't the right fit?
You can switch therapists anytime, at no extra cost. Finding the right match matters, and we make it easy to try someone new until you find your person.
