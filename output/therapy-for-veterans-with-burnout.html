The Weight That Follows You Home

You're used to running on high alert. To managing crisis. To pushing through exhaustion because the mission came first. That mode kept you alive. But in civilian life, it's eating you alive in a different way. You're burned out past the point where a weekend off helps. Sleep doesn't fix it. Days off feel empty because you don't know who you are without the mission anymore.

Maybe you're hypervigilant in ways that make normal workplaces feel strange. Maybe you've thrown yourself into a civilian job thinking structure would help, but instead you're burned out faster than you expected. Or maybe you're quietly struggling—still functional, still showing up, but completely depleted inside. The guilt makes it worse: you survived, you made it home, so why does everything feel this hard?

I thought I was just tired. Turns out I was running on fumes and didn't even know how to stop.

The truth is, your nervous system learned to operate at a level most people never experience. Transition isn't just about finding a new job or settling into a new city. It's about relearning how to exist when you're not in survival mode. That's not something willpower fixes. That's something you need help with—real, expert help from someone who understands both the weight you carried and how to set it down.

Why Civilian Life Feels Impossible, and Why Therapy Actually Works

Burnout after service isn't the same as regular job stress. You're not just tired from work. You're carrying the weight of experiences that fundamentally changed how your brain processes safety, purpose, and exhaustion. Your body learned to exist in a state of readiness that served you then but destroys you now. The gap between who you were and who you're trying to become? That gap is real, and it's not your fault.

Therapy with someone trained to work with veterans gives you language for what you're experiencing and tools to rewire how your body responds to this new civilian life. It's not about forgetting what you lived through. It's about helping your nervous system understand that the constant vigilance, the drive to perform, the sense that rest is weakness—those don't serve you anymore. A therapist who gets military service helps you transition not just your job, but your entire way of being in the world.

Questions people ask before starting

Will a civilian therapist actually understand what I'm dealing with?
That's why we match you with therapists trained specifically in military transition and veteran burnout. They understand the neurological impact of service and why civilian life can feel impossible even when you're technically 'fine.' You can switch therapists anytime at no cost if the fit isn't right.
I'm functioning. I show up to work. Is therapy really necessary?
Functioning and thriving are two different things. You can be functional and still exhausted at your core, still carrying weight that's crushing you slowly. Burnout past exhaustion often hides behind a facade of 'I'm doing okay.' Real change starts when you stop just surviving and actually address what service took from you.
How much does this cost, and can I do it weekly?
Online therapy through BetterHelp starts at just $60-90 per week depending on your therapist, and yes, you can absolutely do weekly sessions. We offer 20% off your first month so you can try it without the financial weight adding to everything else you're carrying.
Will therapy actually change how burned out I feel?
Yes, but not through talking alone. A trauma-informed therapist works with your nervous system directly—helping your body learn that constant vigilance isn't necessary anymore. Most veterans report feeling tangible shifts in exhaustion and emotional clarity within 4-6 weeks of consistent work.
What if I try a therapist and we don't click?
You can switch to a different therapist anytime with no penalty or extra cost. The relationship is everything in therapy, and a good therapist won't take it personally. Finding the right fit is part of the process, not a failure.
If you are in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 immediately — the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. BetterHelp is not a crisis service.
