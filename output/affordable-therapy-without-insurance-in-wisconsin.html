Therapy shouldn't require a second mortgage

The therapy cost crisis is real. In-person therapists in Wisconsin often charge $150 to $250 per session after insurance. Out of pocket, that adds up to thousands a year. For someone making $35,000 annually, that's not a reasonable choice—it's a door locked by money. The system assumes you either have insurance or unlimited funds. Most people have neither.

What many Wisconsinites don't know: online therapy costs significantly less. No overhead. No office rent passed to you. No insurance company middleman taking a cut. That means therapists can charge less and still build sustainable practices. You're not settling for second-rate care—you're accessing the same licensed professionals at prices that actually fit a real life. Sessions start at around $60–$90 per week. First month discounts bring that down even further.

What helps

Therapy works. Study after study shows that consistent care—whether in-person or online—helps people manage anxiety, depression, grief, and life transitions. For people without insurance in Wisconsin, online therapy removes one barrier: cost. That opens the door to everything else: healing, clarity, better sleep, real change.

Questions people ask before starting

Is online therapy actually legit, or is it just someone texting me advice?
Online therapists are fully licensed—the same credentials as in-person therapists. You have video sessions where you see and hear your therapist in real time. It's not chat-based self-help. It's real therapy, minus the office and the price markup.
Will my therapist judge me for not having insurance or money?
No. Therapists know that financial stress is part of real life. They're trained to meet you where you are. Many therapists chose online platforms specifically to make care more accessible. You're not a burden for needing affordable options.
How much would this actually cost me per week?
Pricing typically runs $60–$90 per week depending on your therapist. Many platforms offer first-month discounts around 20% off, bringing that first payment closer to $50–$70. That's manageable for most household budgets.
Does online therapy actually work, or is it just a Band-Aid?
Research shows online therapy is just as effective as in-person for anxiety, depression, and most common concerns. Consistency matters more than location. If you show up weekly and engage honestly, you'll see real shifts.
What if I get a therapist and we don't click?
You can switch therapists anytime, free of charge. No penalty. No guilt. Finding the right fit sometimes takes one or two attempts. That's normal and expected. The platform makes it easy.
If you are in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 immediately — the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. BetterHelp is not a crisis service.
