Questions people ask before starting

Is online therapy as real as sitting in an office?
Yes. The relationship you build with your therapist is what matters, not the room. Video sessions let you be in a space where you're comfortable—your home, somewhere safe. Research shows it's equally effective for anxiety, depression, and most mental health concerns.
What if I can't afford even $120 a week?
Start with what you can actually do. Many therapists work with sliding scale fees if you ask, and online platforms often offer discounts or financial assistance. You might also find community mental health centers in Maryland that serve uninsured patients on a sliding scale.
How much does this actually cost each week, and is there a discount?
Most licensed online therapists in Maryland charge between $90–$160 per week depending on experience and your location. Many offer 20% off your first month to help you get started without a huge upfront investment.
Will therapy actually help, or will I waste money?
It works if you show up and do the work. You're not paying for a magic fix. You're paying for someone trained to listen deeply, ask the right questions, and help you see patterns you couldn't see alone. Most people notice shifts within 3–4 sessions.
What if I start and don't like my therapist?
You can switch. No contracts, no guilt, no penalty. Finding the right fit matters. If someone isn't a match, you tell your platform and get matched with someone new. That freedom is actually built in.
If you are in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 immediately — the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. BetterHelp is not a crisis service.
