What People Actually Love (and Actually Hate)

The biggest wins people report: flexibility that actually works. You message your therapist when you need to—3 AM panic, Tuesday lunch break, Sunday afternoon dread. No waiting for the next appointment slot. For people with unpredictable schedules, severe anxiety about phone calls, or who live in areas with few therapists, this changes everything. And the price point, especially the first month at 20% off, makes therapy feel less like a luxury.

What frustrates people most: the luck of the draw with therapist matching. You might connect with someone brilliant on day one. You might have to switch three times to find your person. Some folks find the app clunky. Others wish there were more video options instead of text-based therapy. And here's the thing nobody talks about—sometimes online therapy just doesn't work for people who need to be in the room with another human. That's not a flaw in BetterHelp. That's just how some of us are wired.

What helps

Therapy—whether online or in-person—works when you do the work. BetterHelp lowers the barriers to getting started. But real change comes from showing up honestly, doing the hard conversations, and staying consistent. It's a tool. A good one, for many people. But you have to use it.

Questions people ask before starting

Won't talking to a stranger online just feel fake?
Sometimes it does at first. The lack of physical presence can feel impersonal. But many people say the distance actually makes it easier to be vulnerable—you're not watching someone's face react in real time. That can be freeing. Give it 3-4 sessions before deciding.
What if the therapist I match with is terrible?
You can switch anytime, no questions asked. BetterHelp makes this actually free and easy, not something you have to fight for. Finding your person might take a few tries. That's normal, even with in-person therapy.
Is it actually affordable, or is the pricing a trick?
Pricing runs roughly $60-90 per week depending on your therapist and plan—significantly less than traditional therapy. First month is 20% off. No hidden fees. If you have insurance that covers telehealth, you may be able to use those benefits with BetterHelp.
Will it actually change anything, or will I just vent to my phone?
Online therapy works about as well as in-person therapy for most conditions, according to research. The difference is you. A good therapist gives you tools and perspective, but you have to use them. BetterHelp can't force that. You have to want to do the work.
What if I realize after two weeks that I hate it?
You can cancel anytime, even mid-month. BetterHelp's refund policy is straightforward. No lengthy contract, no penalty. If it's not working, you're not locked in.
If you are in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 immediately — the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. BetterHelp is not a crisis service.
