Questions people ask before starting

Will a therapist actually take me seriously if I'm paying out of pocket?
Completely. Licensed therapists on platforms like BetterHelp aren't gatekeeping care based on your insurance card—they're there because they want to help. You get the same training, credentials, and professional standards as any therapist in an office. Your commitment matters more than your payment method.
What if I start therapy and realize it's not helping?
You can switch therapists anytime, at no cost. This isn't a contract. If the fit isn't right, you find someone else. Most people try 1-3 therapists before landing on someone who clicks. That's normal, and the platform handles it seamlessly.
How much does it actually cost per week?
Most online therapy platforms charge $60-90 per week for consistent sessions. Many offer 20% off your first month, and some have sliding scales if you're in genuine financial hardship. That's less than a therapy copay used to cost, and you're not out anything for waitlists or no-shows.
Is online therapy really as effective as in-person?
Research says yes—especially for anxiety, depression, and stress. The therapeutic relationship is what heals, not the room you're in. Many people actually open up more online, in their own space. You get licensed therapy without the commute, the cost, or the waiting room anxiety.
What if I'm worried about privacy or confidentiality?
Licensed therapists are bound by the same confidentiality laws online as in-person. Your sessions are encrypted, your records are protected, and they can't share anything without your permission. You're as legally protected as you would be anywhere else.
If you are in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 immediately — the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. BetterHelp is not a crisis service.
