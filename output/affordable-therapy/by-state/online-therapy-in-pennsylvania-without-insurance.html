Questions people ask before starting

Is online therapy actually effective, or am I just settling?
Online therapy is just as effective as in-person therapy for most people. Studies consistently show that what matters is the connection with your therapist and your commitment to the work—not whether you're in an office or talking from home.
How much does this actually cost without insurance?
Most licensed therapists working directly online charge between $60–$120 per session. Many offer packages or discounts. We offer a 20% discount on your first month, bringing typical weekly sessions down to what some people spend on coffee and streaming services combined.
What if I don't click with my therapist?
You can switch to a different therapist anytime, at no penalty and no extra charge. Finding the right fit sometimes takes a session or two, and that's completely normal. Your comfort and trust matter—we built the system around that.
Will a therapist actually be licensed, or am I getting some app substitute?
You'll work with a fully licensed therapist (LPC, LCSW, or psychologist) who meets Pennsylvania's requirements. No AI, no life coaches, no shortcuts. Real credentials. Real training. Real accountability.
Do I need to commit to long-term therapy I can't afford?
No. You can start with weekly sessions and adjust anytime based on your budget and needs. Some people come for six weeks to work through a specific issue. Others continue longer. It's your choice, every single time.
