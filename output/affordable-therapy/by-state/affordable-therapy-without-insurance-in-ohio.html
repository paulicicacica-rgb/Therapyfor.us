Questions people ask before starting

Can I really get good therapy without paying hundreds per session?
Yes. Online therapy platforms in Ohio offer sessions at $60–$120 per week, often lower than traditional in-person therapists. Quality doesn't require premium pricing. You'll work with licensed therapists who are there because they believe therapy should be accessible.
Won't therapy be awkward if I'm doing it online instead of in an office?
Most people find online therapy easier, not harder. You're in your own space, more comfortable, less travel time, and often more open about what you're really feeling. The connection with your therapist is what matters—the screen disappears fast.
How much am I actually looking at per week, and are there payment plans?
Weekly sessions average $60–$120 depending on your therapist. Many platforms offer 20% off your first month, and most accept sliding scale pricing. Some offer monthly payment plans so it feels manageable alongside rent and everything else.
What if therapy doesn't help? Am I throwing money away?
Therapy works when the fit is right. You'll notice shifts within 3–4 weeks: better sleep, clearer thinking, less weight on your chest. If it's not working, you'll know because you'll feel different—not perfect, but genuinely better.
What if I don't click with my therapist? Can I switch?
Absolutely. Most online platforms let you switch therapists anytime, free of charge. Finding the right fit matters. If the first one isn't clicking, you try another. No penalty. No guilt. Just finding the person who gets you.
If you are in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 immediately — the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. BetterHelp is not a crisis service.
