Questions people ask before starting

Will online therapy actually work if I can't see someone in person?
Yes. Research shows online therapy is just as effective as in-person for most people. The relationship with your therapist—their ability to listen and understand—doesn't depend on being in the same room. Many people find online therapy easier to be honest in because there's less pressure.
What if I start and realize it's not helping?
You can switch therapists anytime for free. There's no contract, no penalty. Finding the right fit matters, and BetterHelp makes it possible to try again without it costing you extra money or time.
How much does this actually cost per week?
Pricing starts around $60-$90 per week for most Illinois residents, with sessions happening weekly. New members get 20% off their first month, which softens the initial investment. You can also adjust your plan or pause anytime.
What if I can't afford even that right now?
BetterHelp has financial assistance options and sliding scale pricing for those who qualify. There's also flexibility to start with bi-weekly sessions instead of weekly, then adjust as your situation changes. The goal is to make this work for where you are, not where you wish you were financially.
Won't my employer or someone find out I'm in therapy?
Your therapy is completely private. You're paying out of pocket, not through insurance, so there's no paperwork attached to your employer or anyone else. Sessions are between you and your therapist—full stop.
If you are in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 immediately — the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. BetterHelp is not a crisis service.
