Questions people ask before starting

Is online therapy actually legal in Wisconsin without insurance?
Absolutely. Licensed therapists in Wisconsin can practice online and see clients directly—no insurance required. You're paying the therapist, not an insurance company. It's straightforward and completely legitimate.
How do I know the therapist is actually licensed?
Reputable platforms like BetterHelp verify every therapist's credentials before they're listed. You can see their license, specialties, and client reviews. You're getting a real, licensed professional—the same person you'd see in a physical office.
What does it actually cost per week?
Most online therapy in Wisconsin runs $60-120 per week, depending on your therapist and plan. Many platforms offer 20% off your first month. That's often less than a single in-person session, and you get weekly consistency.
Will it actually help, or is this just a Band-Aid?
Therapy works because it creates space to process what's happening and build real skills—not because of where it happens. Studies show online therapy is equally effective for most people. The key is showing up consistently, and that's easier when it fits your life.
What if I get a therapist and we don't click?
You can switch anytime, free of charge. No contracts, no penalties. Finding the right fit matters, and good platforms make it easy to keep looking until you find someone you trust.
If you are in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 immediately — the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. BetterHelp is not a crisis service.
