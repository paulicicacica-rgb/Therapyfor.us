Questions people ask before starting

Will a therapist really work with me if I don't have insurance?
Absolutely. Many therapists actively choose to work outside insurance specifically to keep costs low and stay in control of their practice. You'll find therapists in Tennessee who specialize in exactly this—no insurance, transparent pricing, no hidden fees.
What if I can barely afford anything right now?
Start with free resources while you explore options, or look for therapists offering truly sliding scale rates based on your actual income. Some also offer payment plans. Your first conversation about affordability is free—no judgment.
How much does it actually cost per week?
Most online therapists in Tennessee without insurance range from $30-80 per session, and many offer 20% off your first month. That's roughly $120-320 monthly for weekly sessions. Some practices also offer 15-minute check-ins between full sessions at lower rates.
Can online therapy actually work, or am I just wasting money?
Research shows online therapy is as effective as in-person for most people. The key is finding the right therapist, and you can switch freely if the match isn't working—no commitment required.
What if I start therapy and realize my therapist isn't a good fit?
You can switch to a different therapist anytime, at no cost. Fit matters more than anything else. Most platforms let you try someone new immediately if the first session doesn't click.
If you are in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 immediately — the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. BetterHelp is not a crisis service.
