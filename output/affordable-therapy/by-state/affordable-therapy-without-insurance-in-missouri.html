Questions people ask before starting

Will a therapist actually take me seriously if I'm not insured?
Absolutely. Online therapists who work with uninsured patients are used to serving people who are managing their finances carefully. They respect your decision to prioritize your mental health and treat you with the same professionalism they'd offer anyone else. Your worth as a patient has nothing to do with your insurance status.
How much does therapy actually cost without insurance in Missouri?
Online therapy typically starts around $60–90 per week, depending on the therapist and platform. Many offer a 20% discount on your first month, bringing initial costs down even further. That's roughly $240–360 monthly—less than most people spend on streaming services combined, and infinitely more valuable to your mental health.
Will online therapy actually help, or am I just paying for someone to listen?
Online therapy is backed by solid research showing it works as well as in-person care for anxiety, depression, and most other concerns. You're not paying for listening—you're paying for training, expertise, and someone who knows how to help you rewire patterns that have been stuck. Real change happens through structured work, not just venting.
What if I find a therapist but we don't click?
You can switch anytime, free of charge. Most platforms make it easy to try a different therapist if the first one isn't right. Finding the right fit matters, and good providers know this—they'd rather you find someone better for your specific needs than stay in a mediocre match.
Is this actually confidential without insurance companies involved?
Yes. Your privacy is protected by the same laws—therapist-patient confidentiality applies whether you're insured or not. Some online platforms use encrypted messaging and secure video. You can ask directly about their privacy practices during your first conversation. You're in control.
If you are in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 immediately — the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. BetterHelp is not a crisis service.
