Questions people ask before starting

Is online therapy actually legit if I don't have insurance?
Yes. Licensed therapists in Maryland can see you online through regulated platforms. It's not a loophole—it's a legitimate way to access care. The therapist is real, licensed, and bound by the same confidentiality laws as in-person therapy.
What if I can't afford even $70 a week?
Many platforms offer sliding scale pricing, meaning they adjust costs based on what you actually earn. Some start at $40-50 weekly. You can also talk directly with your therapist about what works for your budget—many will work with you.
How much does therapy actually cost without insurance in Maryland?
Most online therapy through platforms like BetterHelp runs $60-90 per week for unlimited messaging and weekly sessions. That's roughly $240-360 monthly. Many offer 20% off your first month, bringing your first session cost way down.
Will therapy actually help, or am I just paying to talk to someone?
Therapy isn't just venting. Evidence-based approaches like CBT, DBT, and talk therapy literally change brain patterns. People see real shifts in anxiety, mood, sleep, and relationships—usually within 4-8 weeks of consistent sessions.
What if I get matched with a therapist I don't click with?
You can switch anytime, free of charge. No contracts, no fees, no guilt. Finding the right fit matters, and reputable platforms make it easy to change if needed.
If you are in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 immediately — the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. BetterHelp is not a crisis service.
