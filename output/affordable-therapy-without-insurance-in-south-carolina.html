Questions people ask before starting

If I'm uninsured, do I have to pay full price upfront?
No. Online therapy platforms offer weekly rates—typically $60-$90 per week—paid as you go. You're never paying for months upfront. Many offer 20% off your first month, making the entry point even easier. You control when you start and stop.
Will a therapist really take me seriously if I'm uninsured?
Absolutely. Licensed therapists working online chose that model specifically to serve people without traditional insurance. Your therapist cares about your mental health, not your insurance status. Many have personal experience navigating access barriers themselves.
Is online therapy actually as effective as in-person?
Research says yes, especially for anxiety, depression, and relationship issues. You get the same licensed professional, the same therapeutic techniques—just without commuting or waitlists. Some people actually feel more comfortable opening up in their own home.
What if the first therapist isn't a good fit?
You can switch anytime, at no penalty and no extra cost. Finding the right therapist matters, and no platform should trap you. Most people find someone great on their first or second try—and knowing you can switch takes the pressure off.
How do I know this is actually legitimate therapy and not a scam?
All therapists on reputable platforms are licensed, verified, and meet state requirements—including South Carolina's. Check that you're using a platform with clear credentials listed for each therapist. BetterHelp therapists have visible qualifications and reviews from real clients.
If you are in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 immediately — the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. BetterHelp is not a crisis service.
