Questions people ask before starting

Isn't therapy without insurance more expensive, not less?
Actually, it's often the opposite. When therapists don't bill insurance, they cut overhead. No administrative staff, no coding delays. Many charge $60–90 weekly for uninsured clients versus $150+ copays. You're paying one person directly, not funding an insurance machine.
Will a therapist actually take a cash-pay client in Colorado?
Yes. Many Colorado therapists prefer it. They get paid immediately, you get lower rates, and no insurance denial letters. Online platforms specifically build this model. You'll find hundreds of licensed therapists in Colorado who work this way every day.
How much is therapy each week without insurance?
Weekly sessions typically run $60–90 per week with online platforms. Many offer 20% off your first month, bringing first sessions to under $50. You control how often you go—weekly, every other week, whenever fits your budget and needs.
Will therapy actually help if I don't have much money?
Therapy works because of the conversation, not the price. Your therapist's training, your willingness to show up, and the trust you build together matter far more than the invoice. Some of the most meaningful breakthroughs happen in $60 sessions.
What if I find a therapist and we don't click?
You can switch anytime, free, with zero penalty. Most platforms let you message a new therapist the next day. Finding the right person matters—platforms know this. They make switching easy because they know it's worth it when you find your match.
If you are in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 immediately — the Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, available 24 hours a day in English and Spanish. BetterHelp is not a crisis service.
